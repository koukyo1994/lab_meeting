Job hunting in Japan

Japanese Employment System

Hiring new graduates simultaneously

  • Employ new graduates at once
  • Basically internships exprerience is not needed
  • Usually start from the lowest position

Pros and Cons

Merit Demerit
Employer
  • Enclose excellent talent
  • Reduction of labor and cost
  • Fit to the social system
  • Mid-career adoption system does not develop
Employee
  • Easy to work in large companies
  • Skills/Experience not needed
  • Hard to get out of the rail
  • Ability is often neglected
  • Need to apply many companies in a short period

Job hunting in USA

American Employment System

Skill based hiring

  • All year recruitment
  • Skills/Experience matter a lot
  • Chance to start from high salary

Pros and Cons

Merit Demerit
Empoyer
  • Can get skilled employee
  • Employees are likely to change jobs
  • Internships should be educational
Employee
  • Have many chances to get a job if skilled
  • Negotiation is possible
  • What they've learned is important
  • Need to study hard at university
  • Academic background is important

What made the difference?

Social system in Japan

Japanese social system is composed of 2 systems

  • Lifetime employment
  • Seniority system
  • (Additionally) Retiring Age

Pros and Cons

Merit Demerit
Lifetime Employment
  • Low unemployment rate
  • Team Work
  • Decline in labor productivity
  • Rampant of irregular employment
Seniority
  • Encourage the middle age
  • Discourage the younger age
